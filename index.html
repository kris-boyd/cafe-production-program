Fay Nass

Fay Nass is a queertheatre-maker, director, writer, curator, producer, educator, scholar, innovator and tireless advocate for diversity and inclusion. Her practice is dedicated to creating artful, truthful nterpretations of gender,race, diaspora and cultural identity. Fay’s work has been presented locally, nationally and internationally.Fay is currently the Artistic Director of the Frank Theatre Company, Vancouver.

Chelsea Haberlin

Chelsea is the Artistic Director of Neworld Theatre and Associate Artist with ITSAZOO Productions, which she co-founded in 2006. As a director she has worked with: ITSAZOO Productions, Neworld Theatre, Arts Club Theatre, Firehall Arts Centre, Theatre SKAM, Pacific Theatre, Delinquent Theatre, Studio 58, and many more. Chelsea has an MFA in directing from UBC and a BFA from UVic. She is the recipient of the Sydney J Risk award in directing, the Ray Michal award for outstanding body of work by an emerging director and a three-time winner and six-time Jessie Richardson Award nominee for Outstanding Direction. (Instagram: @chelseahaberlin)

Sebastien Archibald

Sebastien is an actor, playwright and Artistic Producer of ITSAZOO Productions based on the stolen and unceded territories of the the xwməθkwəy̓əm (Musqueam), Skwxwú7mesh (Squamish), and Səl̓ílwətaʔ/Selilwitulh (Tsleil-Waututh) Nations, where he lives with his wife and children.ForITSAZOO, Sebastien has created numerous productions, including: The Pipeline Project (co-produced with Savage Society); Hidden (w/ UBC Botanical Gardens); The Competition is Fierce (Jessie nomination Outstanding Script). Playwriting awards include: the Bottle Tree National Playwriting Competition; Theatre BC National Playwriting Competition; Monday Magazine ‘M’ Award. Sebastien holds a BFA from the Phoenix Theatre, University of Victoria. Mostrecently you saw him in Redbone Coonhound at the Arts Club.

Paige Louter

Paige is a theatre creator hailing from the traditional territories of the Erie, Neutral, Huron-Wendat, Haudenosaunee and Mississaugas (Hamilton), with an MA in Theatre from the National University of Ireland. With a background in administrative and production support forfestivals (the Galway International Arts Festival, the VancouverFringe Festival), theatres (Pacific Theatre, the Firehall Arts Centre), arts organizations, and non-profits, she’s used to handling details, deadlines, and budgets. She’s committed to the lifelong process of learning and unlearning in the context of equity, justice, and accessibility, and is grateful to many, many teachers along the way. Recent credits include co-producing and acting in The Wolves (With a Spoon /Rumble, Jessie Nomination: Outstanding Production), Danny and the Deep Blue Sea (Stone’s Throw), and The Human Ear(Untold Wants), and performing in The Lonesome West (Cave Canem), Tiny Replicas (Rumble) and Twelfth Night (Tottering Biped).

Claire Love Wilson

Claire Love Wilson is queer white/Scottish settler multidisciplinary artist living on the ancestral and unceded territories of the xwməθkwəy̓əm (Musqueam), Skwxwú7mesh (Squamish), and Səl̓ílwətaʔ/Selilwitulh (Tsleil- Waututh)First Nations. Claire has been featured as a creator and performer at the VancouverFringe Festival, the rEvolverfestival, The ShiftFestival, and Theatre Underthe Gun and in collaboration with The Only Animal, Urban Ink, and the frank theatre. Claire has also facilitated workshops on her unique loop-based creation practice in Vancouver,London, Edinburgh, and Paris. Claire is the co- creator and lead performer of the experimental Scottish-ballad musical “Morag, You’re a Long Time Deid", which premiered in association with Touchstone Theatre in 2022.

Piotr Wieczorek

Piotr Wieczorek (he/him/his) is a recording producer and engineer originally from Warsaw, Poland. Before moving to Canada, he was a long-time collaborator of the pianist and composer Hania Rani; in Vancouver, he has been mostly active on the contemporary classical music scene, working alongside producer Denise Ball, recording in locations ranging from the Orpheum to the Chan Center. He is passionate about working on projects that amplify minority voices, particularly those of the 2SLGBTQ+ community.

Cameron Anderson

Cameron Anderson is a professional videographer with a special focus on live performance. As a filmmaker, he has collaborated on several short documentaries, co-directed the short film Dust, and directed the short film Do Not Go. He is the current in-house video director of The Cultch. He has collaborated with several dozen local arts organizations, including Music on Main, Theatre Replacement, and Green Thumb Theatre. He has also built promotional videos for the Vancouver Green Party, and current BC Green Party leader Sonia Furstenau. He is a graduate of the University of Victoria, and holds a Bachelor of Fine Arts, with distinction. His film training includes a diploma in documentary filmmaking from In Focus Film School, and a year long videography practicum with the Banff Centre for Arts and Creativity.

Kris Boyd

Kris Boyd is a creative technologist, web developer, digital project manager, technical director and audio technician who has been working in Vancouver’s performing arts scene for 25 years. He has been the Project Lead for Digital Storytelling at The Cultch since the project’s inception in 2019. His current work encompasses digitally interactive, immersive and transmedia performance experiences, online video with integrated audience participation components, geo-located audio mobile apps, and virtual world building. Significant recent collaborations include works with Theatre Replacement, Ronnie Burkett, The Transform Festival, Veronique West, Playwrights Theatre Centre, Lili Robinson, and Crow’s Theatre.
